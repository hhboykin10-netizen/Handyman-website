Emergency Shutoff Valve & Supply Line Upgrades

The small water shutoff valves located behind your toilets and underneath your kitchen sinks are your home's first line of defense against a catastrophic plumbing burst. Unfortunately, old-style multi-turn compression valves can sit untouched for years, allowing internal calcium build-up to freeze the stem or dry out the rubber packing washers. When you finally need to turn the knob during an emergency, it will often leak around the handle or refuse to budge entirely.

We solve this vulnerability by replacing fragile, failing multi-turn hardware with high-performance, solid brass quarter-turn ball valves. With a quick 90-degree twist, you can completely isolate water lines to protect your property from sudden water damage.
